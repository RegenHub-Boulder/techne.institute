00 — Public Benefit Purpose

Cultivating scenius

RegenHub, LCA is a Colorado Public Benefit Limited Cooperative Association. The articles of organization were filed February 6, 2026 (Colorado Secretary of State Document #20261163853, ID #20261163853). As a public benefit entity, the cooperative is legally required to balance financial performance against its stated purpose, and to publish an annual benefit report assessing that performance.

Our stated public benefit purpose: to cultivate scenius — the collective intelligence that emerges from sustained proximity of culturally aligned people — by operating as a cooperative venture studio that composes patterns of coordination, care, and value creation with intention and skill, enriching the economic and social habitat of its members, ventures, and community.

We have high conviction in our ability to return value to investors — both financial and multi-capital. Returns are structured across three time horizons: equity (a stake in the cooperative and its venture ecosystem), royalties (ongoing returns from IP created through cooperative work), and patronage (periodic surplus allocation based on multi-capital contribution). The Financial Systems Committee will parameterize these return mechanisms in March 2026.

Investor Members receive economic rights without governance rights. Governance remains democratic: one cooperative member, one vote, regardless of capital contributed. This is not a limitation on investor return — it is the structural choice that makes the cooperative trustworthy enough to generate the returns we're projecting.

04 — Return Structure

Options under Financial Systems Committee review

The Financial Systems Committee (FSC) convenes March 2, 2026, to parameterize return terms. The following options are under consideration. Kevin and Jeremy are comfortable with this open structure — they are investing in alignment and long-term ecosystem health, not a fixed return schedule.

Option A

Capped Revenue Participation

Investor Members receive a percentage of RegenHub's operating revenue — space rental, studio services, venture reciprocity — until a defined cap is reached (e.g., 2× committed capital). After the cap, the membership interest converts to a standard non-economic position. Clean, predictable, no perpetual obligation.

Option B

Retroactive Seed Equity in a Specific Venture

The committed capital is treated as an early-stage investment in one of the founding ventures — Parachute, Postage, Habitat, or a future project — at a seed-stage valuation to be agreed. If the venture achieves exit or significant liquidity, investors participate proportionally. Higher risk and reward than Option A; requires venture founder consent.

Option C

Treated as a Donation

Capital is treated as a founding gift to the cooperative — no financial return expected. Investors receive recognition as founding members and a permanent seat in the cooperative's governance story. Simplest legal structure. Only appropriate if investors have explicitly chosen this framing.

Option D

Full Basket Participation (Long-Term)

Investor Members hold proportionate positions in the basket fund — a unitized stake in the bundled equity of Parachute, Postage, Habitat, and future ventures. Returns flow as ventures appreciate and RegenHub adds equity from its reserve into the basket at higher valuations. This is the aspirational long-term architecture described in the Vision One-Pager. Requires the full Member Agreement instrument to be legal and operationally defined.

The FSC will return a recommendation to the Board no later than March 10, 2026. All Investor Members will be notified and invited to respond before ratification.
