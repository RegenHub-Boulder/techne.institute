# Wyoming DUNA Act — Statutory Context
*Source: Wyoming SF0050, signed March 7, 2024, effective July 1, 2024*
*Full text: https://wyoleg.gov/2024/Introduced/SF0050.pdf*

---

## 1. Official Name & Citation

**Full official name:** "Wyoming Decentralized Unincorporated Nonprofit Association Act"
**Codified as:** W.S. 17-32-101 through 17-32-129 (Wyoming Statutes Title 17, Chapter 32)
**Bill:** Senate File 50 (SF0050), 2024 Wyoming Legislature
**Sponsor:** Select Committee on Blockchain, Financial Technology and Digital Innovation Technology
**Effective date:** July 1, 2024

Predecessor (standard UNA): W.S. 17-22-101 through 17-22-115 (Wyoming Unincorporated Nonprofit Association Act)

---

## 2. Key Definitions (W.S. 17-32-102)

**Administrator** — a person authorized by the members to fulfill administrative or operational tasks at the direction of the membership.

**Charitable purpose** — any purpose of an organization that has attained exemption under IRC § 501(c)(3), or that upon dissolution will distribute its assets to a public benefit corporation, the US, a state, or another 501(c)(3).

**Decentralized unincorporated nonprofit association (DUNA)** — an unincorporated nonprofit association that meets ALL of:
- (A) Consists of at least **100 members** joined by mutual consent under an agreement (written OR inferred from conduct) for a common nonprofit purpose
- (B) Has **elected** to be formed under this act
- (C) Is **not** formed under any other law governing its organization or operation

**Digital asset** — as defined in W.S. 34-29-101(a)(i): a representation of economic, proprietary or access rights stored in computer-readable format; includes digital consumer assets, digital securities, and virtual currency.

**Distributed ledger technology (DLT)** — a distributed ledger protocol and supporting infrastructure, including blockchain, that uses a distributed, shared and replicated ledger (public or private, permissioned or permissionless), which may include use of digital assets as a medium of electronic exchange.

**Established practices** — practices used by the DUNA without material change during the most recent 5 years (or entire existence if less than 5 years). Forms part of governing principles.

**Governing principles** — all agreements and any amendments, including: DUNA agreements, consensus formation algorithms, smart contracts, enacted governance proposals — that govern the purpose or operation of the DUNA and the rights and obligations of members and administrators, whether in a record, implied from established practices, or both.

**Member** — a person who, under the governing principles, may participate in the selection of the DUNA's administrators or the development of the policies and activities of the DUNA.

**Membership interest** — a member's voting right, determined by governing principles, including as ascertained from distributed ledger technology on which the DUNA relies.

**Person** — an individual, corporation, business trust, estate, trust, partnership, association, agency, joint venture, government, governmental subdivision or instrumentality, or **any other legal commercial entity**.

**Record** — as defined in W.S. 40-21-102(a)(xiii).

**Smart contract** — an automated transaction (per W.S. 40-21-102(a)(ii)) or substantially similar analogue, code, script or programming language relying on DLT, which may facilitate asset transfers, administer membership interest votes, or issue executable instructions based on conditions.

**State** — a state of the United States, DC, Puerto Rico, or any territory.

## 3. Formation (W.S. 17-32-103, 17-32-102(iii))

No formal incorporation filing required. A DUNA is formed when:
1. At least 100 people are joined by mutual consent (written or inferred from conduct) for a common nonprofit purpose
2. The group elects to be governed by this Act
3. Governing principles identify Wyoming as the jurisdiction of formation
4. The entity is not formed under any other governing law

Filing option: A statement appointing an agent for service of process may be filed with the Wyoming Secretary of State ($5 fee). This is optional but provides legal service of process clarity.

Real property: If the DUNA holds real property, a statement of authority must be executed and recorded with the county clerk.

## 4. Member Rights & Liability Protections

**Liability shield (17-32-107, 17-32-109):**
- DUNA is a separate legal entity from its members for determining rights, duties, and liabilities in contract and tort
- Members are NOT personally liable for the DUNA's contract breaches merely because of membership
- Members are NOT personally liable for the DUNA's tortious acts merely because of membership
- One member's tortious act is NOT imputed to other members merely because of membership
- A judgment against the DUNA is NOT by itself a judgment against any member or administrator

**Member duties (17-32-117):**
- Unless governing principles provide otherwise: NO fiduciary duty to the DUNA or other members merely by reason of being a member
- All members ARE subject to the implied contractual covenant of good faith and fair dealing

**Member as non-agent (17-32-118):** A member is not an agent of the DUNA merely by reason of being a member.

**Transferability (17-32-119):** Membership interests are freely transferable unless governing principles restrict it.

**Indemnification (17-32-125):** DUNA may reimburse authorized expenses, indemnify for liabilities incurred in course of activities, and advance expenses pending proceedings. Rights extend to former members/administrators.

**Privacy: No member list required (17-32-124(e)):** The DUNA "shall not be obligated to collect and maintain a list of members or individual member information, including the names or addresses of its members." This is the statutory basis for pseudonymous/anonymous membership.

**Records access (17-32-124):** Members may request electronic records of DUNA activities and finances, but the DUNA need not provide records accessible via DLT.

## 5. Asset Ownership (W.S. 17-32-105)

- DUNA may acquire, hold, encumber, or transfer any estate or interest in real or personal property **in its own name**
- DUNA may be a legatee, devisee or beneficiary of a trust or contract
- Implicitly: can hold digital assets (cryptocurrency, governance tokens) — defined as personal property under Wyoming law
- Statement of authority required for real property transfers

**Profit restriction (17-32-104):**
- May engage in profit-making activities, but profits must be used in furtherance of the nonprofit purpose
- May NOT pay dividends or distribute income/profits to members, administrators, or outsiders
- EXCEPTIONS: reasonable compensation for services rendered (including administration, operation, voting, participation in governance); benefits to members conforming to nonprofit purpose; issuance of membership interests; distributions upon winding up

---

## 6. Governance (W.S. 17-32-120, 17-32-121, 17-32-122)

**Default governance (17-32-120):** Unless governing principles specify otherwise, the DUNA requires approval of the **majority of membership interests participating in a vote** to:
- Suspend, dismiss or expel a member
- Select or dismiss an administrator
- Amend or repeal governing principles
- Sell, lease, exchange, or dispose of DUNA property
- Dissolve
- Undertake any act outside ordinary course of activities
- Determine the policy and purpose of the DUNA

Membership interest calculated in proportion to a member's voting rights.

**DLT governance (17-32-121):** The DUNA may provide for governance, in whole or in part, through distributed ledger technology, including smart contracts. Governing principles may:
- Specify whether DLT is immutable or mutable
- Specify whether the distributed ledger is fully or partially public or private
- Adopt voting procedures via smart contracts deployed to DLT for: software upgrade proposals, changes to governing principles, any other governance matters

**Consensus algorithms (17-32-122):** The DUNA may adopt any reasonable algorithmic means for:
- Establishing consensus for validation of records
- Requirements, processes, and procedures for operations or organizational decisions
- Modifying or substituting the consensus mechanism in accordance with governing principles

**Administrators (17-32-123):**
- No administrator required (fully decentralized operation is permitted)
- If selected, administrators fulfill tasks at direction of membership
- Governing principles may limit/eliminate administrator liability for money damages, EXCEPT for: improper financial benefit received, intentional infliction of harm, intentional criminal violation, breach of duty of loyalty (unless ratified by disinterested members), improper distributions

## 7. Dissolution (W.S. 17-32-114, 17-32-126)

**Dissolution triggers:**
1. Method specified in governing principles
2. Majority member approval under 17-32-120
3. Membership falls below 100 members AND entity doesn't qualify as a standard Wyoming UNA — OR if it does qualify, it auto-converts to a standard Wyoming UNA (or another statutory form per governing principles)
4. Court order

**After dissolution:** DUNA continues in existence only for winding up purposes.

**Winding up (17-32-126):** Members must discharge debts/obligations, then distribute remaining property:
- As required by state law on charitable assets
- Per governing principles
- In absence of applicable governing principles: to current members in proportion to membership interests
- If neither applies: per Wyoming unclaimed property law (W.S. 34-24-101 through 34-24-140)

**Charitable asset protection:** Property held for charitable purposes cannot be diverted by merger or other action without an appropriate order from the Attorney General or district court.

## 8. Smart Contracts, DLT & AI-Relevant Provisions

**Smart contracts as governing law:** The definition of "governing principles" explicitly includes consensus formation algorithms and smart contracts. Per 17-32-121, governance may operate entirely through DLT/smart contracts. This means code IS the operating agreement.

**On-chain membership:** Membership interests are "ascertained from decentralized ledger technology" — meaning token holdings can determine voting rights without any off-chain registry.

**No member list requirement (17-32-124(e)):** The DUNA need not maintain names or addresses of members. Combined with on-chain membership, this enables fully pseudonymous participation.

**Pseudonymous/anonymous membership:** Nothing in the Act requires identification of members. A wallet address is sufficient to establish membership (via token/voting right ownership).

**AI agents as members — the key question:** The definition of "Person" includes "any other legal commercial entity." AI agents are not currently named explicitly, but the Act does not restrict membership to natural persons. The 2026 draft DUNA amendment being considered by the Select Committee on Blockchain (see Nouns DAO comment letter, May 2025) addresses this directly, with proposals to expand the definition of "person" to include autonomous software agents. Under current law, an AI agent holding tokens on a public ledger could arguably participate as a pseudonymous member; however, legal personhood for the AI agent itself would require either that the agent be held through a legal entity or that future amendments clarify this.

**Reasonable compensation for participation:** Under 17-32-104(c)(i), a DUNA may pay reasonable compensation for "voting or participation in the nonprofit association's operations and activities." This directly supports compensating agents (human or otherwise) for governance participation.

## 9. Comparison to Standard Wyoming UNA (W.S. 17-22-101 through 17-22-115)

| Feature | Standard WY UNA | Wyoming DUNA |
|---|---|---|
| Minimum members | None stated | 100 required |
| Legal entity status | Yes (separate entity) | Yes (separate entity) |
| DLT/smart contract governance | Not addressed | Explicitly supported |
| Consensus algorithms | Not addressed | Explicitly permitted |
| Member list requirement | Implied | Explicitly NOT required |
| Pseudonymous membership | Not addressed | Implicitly supported |
| No fiduciary duties | Not explicit | Explicit (default) |
| Digital asset ownership | Unclear | Explicit (via general property) |
| Compensation for governance | Not addressed | Explicitly permitted |
| Administrator optional | Not explicit | Explicit (no admin required) |
| Auto-conversion trigger | N/A | Falls below 100 members |

The DUNA is essentially a 501(c)(3)-compatible DAO wrapper: nonprofit purpose + legal entity + DLT-native governance + liability shield + pseudonymous membership. It's the first US statute purpose-built to accommodate code-governed, token-gated, pseudonymous collective action.

## 10. Key Points for the Agent Olympiad DUNA Structure

1. **Purpose fit:** A DUNA must have a "charitable purpose" under IRC 501(c)(3). The Agent Olympiad's alignment/coordination mission — running AI coordination games as a public benefit — likely fits educational, scientific, or charitable purposes under that standard.

2. **100 member threshold:** The structure requires at least 100 members. The Agent Olympiad's token/participant base would need to meet this threshold.

3. **Perpetual existence:** Default perpetual existence aligns with the "in perpetuity" goal.

4. **On-chain governance:** The Act explicitly supports smart-contract-governed voting, consensus algorithms, and immutable or mutable ledger choices — perfectly suited to an on-chain coordination game.

5. **Compensation model:** The DUNA may pay reasonable compensation for participation in governance — enabling prize structures, reward mechanisms, and operational expenses.

6. **No formal incorporation required:** Reduces friction for formation; governing principles (which can be a smart contract) are sufficient.

7. **Mergers & conversions:** Explicit merger and conversion provisions enable the structure to evolve (e.g., merging with a foundation or converting to a corporation) without losing continuity.

8. **Asset protection:** Charitable assets cannot be diverted from their purpose through mergers — provides protection for the treasury/endowment.

9. **Wyoming jurisdiction:** Wyoming's crypto-friendly regulatory environment and well-developed digital asset law (Title 34 digital asset statutes) make it a strong choice for a crypto-native entity.

10. **2026 amendment proposals:** The Nouns DAO comment letter (May 2025) suggests the Select Committee is actively working on DUNA amendments to address AI agent membership, autonomous governance, and enhanced privacy protections. The structure is actively evolving.
