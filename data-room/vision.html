00 — Public Benefit Purpose

Cultivating scenius

Scenius — the collective intelligence that emerges from sustained proximity of culturally aligned people — is the rarest and most durable source of value in the knowledge economy. The greatest creative periods in history weren't produced by lone geniuses. They were produced by scenes: dense networks of people who saw each other regularly, shared references, challenged each other's work, and built on each other's discoveries in real time.

This is RegenHub's public benefit purpose, as filed with the Colorado Secretary of State on February 6, 2026 (Document #20261163853): to cultivate the conditions under which scenius can emerge and flourish.

We pursue this through three layers: physical space at 1515 Walnut St where proximity happens; a shared pattern library where accumulated knowledge becomes a commons; and economic infrastructure that makes multi-capital contribution visible and distributable — rewarding not just financial investment, but labor, expertise, relationships, and community-building.

We are a public benefit entity — and we have high conviction in the economic case. Scenius produces outsized returns precisely because it is rare. The organizations that emerge from a genuine scene carry context, relationships, and trust that cannot be acquired. We are an infrastructure investment in the conditions that produce those organizations.

Returns are structured across three time horizons: equity (a permanent stake in the cooperative and its venture ecosystem), royalties (ongoing returns from IP created through cooperative work), and patronage (periodic allocation of surplus based on multi-capital contribution). This is the economic memory system — how the cooperative recognizes not just current contribution, but durable value created.

01 — The Model

A CSA for Boulder ventures

Community Supported Agriculture works because investors buy a share of the season's harvest upfront — not a single crop, but the whole farm's yield. What if investment in early-stage ventures worked the same way?

Instead of a SAFE into one company at a fixed valuation, you are buying into a basket: equity from multiple ventures incubating at RegenHub, bundled into a single investable share. New ventures are added as they mature. The basket grows. Your position compounds across the whole ecosystem rather than riding a single bet.

This is not the hyper-scale VC model where one in fifteen survives. It is a balanced growth model — one where health and growth are tracked together, and where the success of each venture strengthens every other.

"RegenHub is the soil. Each venture is a seed. The basket is the seasonal harvest — what grows, what matures, what gets added each year. You are investing in the farm."

03 — The Structure

How equity flows into the basket

Each founding venture — Parachute, Postage, Habitat — contributes 10% of their equity to RegenHub as a gift to the cooperative. This is not a sale. It is a commitment: the founders choosing to root their ventures in this ecosystem and share in its collective success.

RegenHub does not put all of that equity into the basket. It puts 1–2% of each venture into the basket fund, making those shares available to external investors. The remaining 8–9% stays on RegenHub's balance sheet — a growing reserve that becomes more valuable as the ventures mature.

This reserve is the engine of the model. As ventures appreciate, RegenHub can add further equity from its reserve into the basket, raising new capital at higher valuations without any new dilution to the founders. Each addition refreshes the fund and creates new opportunities for investors to participate.

Techne Institute sits outside this structure entirely — it is becoming a nonprofit, funded by grants and tuition. Techne is not in the basket. It is the reason the basket keeps producing.

04 — The Investment

What this transaction looks like

This is the first event of reciprocity — the first harvest. You are not buying into a cap table. You are buying a share of the basket: a unitized stake in the bundled equity of Parachute, Postage, and Habitat, with the expectation that new ventures will be added in future seasons.

Your Position

$50k each — $100k in committed capital — sits alongside equity contributions from the founding ventures, targeting a starting fund value of $250k–$500k. You do not need to fully subscribe the fund; any unsubscribed value accrues to the co-op, strengthening each member's position and available for future initiatives.

What You Receive

A proportional share of the basket fund. As ventures in the basket grow, your share grows with them. As new ventures are added, the basket value increases. As RegenHub's cooperative treasury holds basket shares, the whole ecosystem's growth is reflected in your position.

How It Grows

Say the basket starts at $250k and appreciates to $5m as the ventures mature. Meanwhile Parachute has grown to a $100m valuation — meaning RegenHub's 10% stake is worth $10m, of which 2% is already in the basket ($2m) and 8% remains on the balance sheet ($8m). RegenHub now adds another 3% of Parachute from its reserve into the basket — worth $3m at current valuation. The basket grows, the hub raises fresh capital, and your position appreciates alongside it. Nothing about this requires the founders to give up anything more than they already committed.

Multiple Funds

As the basket matures and its entry price rises, new funds can be created for the next generation of ventures emerging from RegenHub — cheaper, earlier, higher risk and reward. The first basket becomes an established fund; the second basket is the frontier. The ecosystem produces a continuous harvest of new opportunities, not a single bet.

Near-Term Use

The immediate priority is securing the Suite 200 lease — the physical home for Techne Institute and the ventures. Beyond that, near-term investments in shared infrastructure (including high-compute local AI capacity) benefit every venture in the basket and increase the value of the whole.
