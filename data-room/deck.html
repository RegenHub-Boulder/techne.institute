01 — The Model

A cooperative venture studio

RegenHub is a Colorado Public Benefit LCA incubating civic technology, AI tools, and bioregional infrastructure at 1515 Walnut St, Suite 200, Boulder. Three founding ventures have each committed 10% of their equity to RegenHub as a cooperative gift. That equity becomes the basket: a bundled, investable stake in the whole ecosystem rather than a single company.

For the full investment thesis — the CSA analogy, equity flow structure, venture profiles, and season-by-season growth scenario — see the Vision One-Pager. This deck covers the place, the operational picture, the legal architecture, and where we're headed.

02 — Public Benefit Purpose

Cultivating scenius

RegenHub is a Colorado Public Benefit Limited Cooperative Association — filed February 6, 2026 (Colorado SOS Document #20261163853). As a public benefit entity, we are legally required to balance financial performance against our stated purpose: cultivating scenius, the collective intelligence that emerges from sustained proximity of culturally aligned people.

This is not altruism. It is a thesis about durable value creation. Organizations that emerge from a genuine scene — with accumulated context, deep relationships, and shared trust — produce better outcomes over longer time horizons than those assembled from scratch. We are building the soil. The ventures are the harvest.

Being a public benefit cooperative means our governance architecture is purpose-built for this: one-member-one-vote regardless of capital invested; surplus flows to contributors based on patronage rather than ownership; and the cooperative is legally accountable to its purpose, not just to financial return. We publish an annual benefit report assessing our performance against that purpose.

For investors: we have high conviction in our ability to return value — both financial and multi-capital. The economic memory system (equity + royalties + patronage) means contribution is recognized across multiple time horizons, not just at the moment of investment.

06 — The Agreement Stack

The legal and economic architecture

Participation is structured as cooperative membership — not a securities offering. Membership interests in a Colorado LCA are actively governed, patronage-based, and democratically controlled. The intent is that no SEC registration or state securities filing is required. All terms confirmed with cooperative attorney Jeff Pote (Pote Law Firm) before any transaction closes.

1

Bylaws — Governance foundation

Establishes member classes, capital account treatment, patronage formula, voting rights. Class 4 (Investor Member) is non-voting — one member, one vote regardless of capital contribution. Multi-class membership is permitted: a member holding any patron class (1–3) alongside Class 4 holds full voting rights through their patron-class interest.

Draft complete
2

Member Agreements — Instrument layer

Per-class operating contracts that define the basket instrument in legal terms: which ventures, what equity percentages, capital account treatment, reserve mechanism, and rights as the basket grows. This is the document that makes the basket legible as a cooperative membership interest rather than a fund share.

Pending attorney draft
3

Term Sheet — First-season transaction

Documents the initial investment event. Intentionally minimal: names the parties, the capital amounts, and establishes that return terms will be determined by the member body (Financial Systems Committee, convening March 2, 2026). No defined return at signing.
