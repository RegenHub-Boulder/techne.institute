Techne at 1515 Walnut

A brief introduction to RegenHub, LCA — prepared for Lynn Paul

Who We Are

RegenHub, LCA is a Colorado Limited Cooperative Association filed in February 2026, doing business as Techne. We are a cooperative venture studio — a small group of builders, designers, and technologists who share space, infrastructure, and a legal structure that lets us launch technology ventures together.

The cooperative has eight founding organizers, including Kevin Owocki (co-founder of Gitcoin, Managing Partner at Allo Capital), Todd Youngblood (Ventures & Operations Steward), and Aaron G Neyer (Financial Systems). Our attorney is Jeff Pote of Pote Law Firm. We operate as a public benefit entity with the stated purpose of cultivating scenius — the creative intelligence that emerges when skilled people share sustained proximity.

What Happens on the Third Floor

The third floor of 1515 Walnut is home to Techne's working studio. Three things happen here daily:

Coworking and studio services. Members and affiliates use the space as their primary workplace. This generates steady monthly revenue through desk memberships, meeting room access, and shared infrastructure.

Technology education. We run cohort-based programs under the name Learn by Build — intensive courses in AI, software development, and civic technology. These bring foot traffic, talent, and tuition revenue into the building.

Venture development. The cooperative incubates early-stage technology ventures. Three are currently active: Parachute (voice-first AI tools), Postage (blockchain-based email filtering), and Habitat (cooperative accounting infrastructure). Each venture contributes a small percentage of equity back to the cooperative, creating a shared basket that compounds over time.

Revenue Model

Techne generates revenue from three sources: space (coworking memberships, event hosting, meeting rooms), services (technology education, studio consulting), and venture reciprocity (a 1% annual commitment from each venture back to the cooperative). The goal is for space and service revenue to cover operating costs, while venture reciprocity creates long-term upside for the cooperative and everyone connected to it.

Why 1515 Walnut

Boulder sits at the intersection of a world-class research university, a deep technology talent pool, and a growing interest in cooperative and civic technology. 1515 Walnut is where this work is already happening. The third floor is not a speculative bet — it is an operating studio with people building every day.

We are seeking a lease arrangement for the third floor that reflects the long-term relationship we hope to build with the building and its owners. We believe there is an opportunity for a partnership that goes beyond a standard landlord-tenant transaction — one where the growth of what happens inside the building benefits everyone connected to it.

What We'd Like to Discuss

We are interested in a conversation about the third floor that starts with straightforward lease terms and explores whether a deeper relationship makes sense for both sides. We have over $100,000 in committed capital from investors who are also participants in the work — including Kevin Owocki, who has operated from 1515 Walnut's second floor for over a year. We have an attorney structuring the cooperative's agreements and a clear operating model. We come prepared and we come in good faith.

We look forward to the conversation.
