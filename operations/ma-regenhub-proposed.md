# REGENHUB, LCA (DBA TECHNE)
## MEMBERSHIP AGREEMENT

This Membership Agreement ("Agreement") is made and entered into as of the Effective Date (as defined below) by and between the undersigned (hereinafter "Applicant") whose address is set forth on the signature page hereto and **RegenHub, LCA**, a Colorado public benefit, limited cooperative association

<!-- PROPOSED: Address updated to Suite 200 (pending investment for third floor) -->
having its principal office at **1515 Walnut St, Suite 200, Boulder, CO 80302** ("Cooperative"),

each a "Party" and collectively the "Parties". Such Applicant has been approved by Cooperative's board of directors ("Board") in accordance with the procedure set forth in Cooperative's bylaws ("Bylaws"), hereby incorporated by reference as if fully set forth herein. Capitalized terms not otherwise defined herein shall have the definitions set forth in the Bylaws.

## RECITALS

<!-- DECIDE: Confirm the public benefit purpose statement — should match language in Articles of Organization exactly. Current proposal: "Cultivating scenius." May require a more descriptive formulation. -->
**WHEREAS**, the Cooperative is a member-owned, public benefit, limited cooperative association committed to **cultivating scenius** — building the physical, legal, and financial infrastructure for a new generation of cooperative ventures rooted in place, and fostering a community of practice where collective intelligence emerges from sustained proximity among values-aligned technologists, builders, and practitioners;

**WHEREAS**, Applicant is an individual or entity who meets the qualifications for membership in the Cooperative as set forth in the Bylaws and desires to become a Member to take advantage of the membership benefits of the Cooperative; and

**WHEREAS**, the Cooperative desires to admit Applicant to Cooperative's membership as a Member, and Applicant desires to be admitted to Cooperative's membership as a Member, in each case in accordance with and subject to the terms and conditions of this Agreement.

NOW THEREFORE, in consideration of the foregoing recitals which are incorporated in this Agreement and the representations, warranties, and covenants set forth below, and for other good and valuable consideration, the receipt and sufficiency of which are hereby acknowledged by the Parties, the Parties, intending to be legally bound, agree as follows:

## 1. Membership, Stock Price, and Dues

<!-- DECIDE: Meadow uses a single Class A stock. RegenHub uses a four-class member structure (Classes 1–4). This section must be adapted per class. The appropriate class is determined at admission. A class-specific version of this agreement (or a class-specific addendum) will be required for each member type. -->
**1.1 Membership.** Applicant, following purchase of the minimum required membership share(s) for each applicable membership class and upon execution of this Agreement and any other further documents required by the Board, will be entitled to be a Member of the Cooperative in the class or classes designated on the signature page.

> **Class 1 — Cooperative Member:** Full governance and patronage participation. Patron member. Voting rights.
> **Class 2 — Coworking Member:** Space and community access. Patron member. Voting rights.
> **Class 3 — Community Participant:** Programming access. Patron member. Voting rights.
> **Class 4 — Investor Member:** Non-voting through this interest alone. Capital account-based. Basket fund participation.

<!-- PROPOSED: Multi-class membership explicitly permitted. An Applicant may hold interests in more than one membership class. Each class carries its own rights, obligations, share price, and dues. Patron rights (including voting) attach to any member holding at least one patron-class interest (Classes 1–3), regardless of whether they also hold a Class 4 interest. -->
**1.1.1 Multi-Class Membership.** A Member may hold interests in more than one membership class simultaneously. Execution of this Agreement and payment of the applicable share price and dues for each class designated on the signature page constitutes admission to each such class. Rights and obligations attach per class. A Member holding any patron-class interest (Class 1, 2, or 3) holds full voting rights in Cooperative governance, regardless of whether they also hold a Class 4 Investor Member interest.

**1.2 Incorporation of Governing Documents and Policies.** Applicant acknowledges receipt of copies of Cooperative's articles of organization and Bylaws (collectively, the "Governing Documents") in effect as of the Effective Date of this Agreement. Applicant shall comply with and be bound by the terms of the Governing Documents, as modified from time to time in accordance with their terms. In accordance with the provisions of the Governing Documents, the officers and directors of Cooperative shall have management control over the activities of Cooperative and may, from time to time, modify, add, or delete Cooperative membership benefits upon notice to Applicant. Applicant further agrees to abide by any and all policies that are now in force or as may be adopted and/or amended from time to time by the Board ("Policies"). Applicant shall promptly notify the Cooperative of any changes that may result in noncompliance with this Agreement, the Governing Documents, or the Policies.

<!-- DECIDE: The services description must be defined per class. Meadow's generic "services to Cooperative" framing works for a single-class co-op; RegenHub's multi-class structure requires specificity. See memberAgreementData.js §1.3. -->
**1.3 Services.** Subject to the Governing Documents and to the member class designation on the signature page, Applicant shall commence providing, or continue to provide, services or participation to the Cooperative in the capacity appropriate to their class:

> **Class 1:** Venture or studio work in service of the cooperative's mission.
> **Class 2:** Coworking presence and community participation.
> **Class 3:** Programming participation and community engagement.
> **Class 4:** Capital contribution per the basket instrument defined in the Member Agreement.

**1.4 Stock Price.** Applicant shall subscribe to and pay for the minimum required share(s) of the applicable membership class as follows:

<!-- DECIDE: Share prices per class to be set in Schedule A. Current proposed ranges: Class 1: $250–500, Class 2: $100–250, Class 3: $0–50, Class 4: TBD (per basket instrument). -->
> **1.4.1 Capital Stock.** Membership share(s) at a price as set forth in Schedule A of the Bylaws for the applicable membership class, due in lump-sum cash payment, by executing a promissory note, or some combination thereof (and where any such promissory note shall be secured by the purchasing Member's Stock until repaid in full), or in any other manner permitted or required by the Board.
>
> **1.4.2 Price per Share.** Applicant acknowledges that the purchase price per share shall be as set forth in Schedule A or by a resolution of the Board, and may be increased or decreased from time to time by the Board.

<!-- DECIDE: Membership dues per class to be finalized in Schedule A. Proposed: Class 1: TBD annual, Class 2: monthly membership fee, Class 3: $0, Class 4: none. -->

The current Cooperative membership dues and fee structure per class are set forth in Schedule A. The Board, in its sole discretion, may amend Schedule A and, accordingly, may add, eliminate, increase or decrease from time to time the membership dues and other fees (collectively, the "Membership Dues Changes"). Applicant hereby agrees to be bound by and timely pay any such Membership Dues Changes applicable to their class.

## 2. Applicant Requirements and Benefits

**2.1 Accuracy of Information.** Applicant represents and warrants to, and covenants with, the Cooperative that all information provided by Applicant, including but not limited to any application documents completed by Applicant, financial statements made by Applicant, or supplemental documents and/or information provided by Applicant is complete, accurate, and truthful.

<!-- DECIDE: Benefits must be defined per class. Meadow's single list (a–d) is adapted here. Item (d) expanded to reflect RegenHub's actual offering. -->
**2.2 Member Benefits Furnished by Cooperative.** The Cooperative intends to provide the following benefits to Applicant and other Members of the applicable class, in each case in accordance with and subject to the Governing Documents and Policies:

(a) ownership of Cooperative as a Member in accordance with and subject to the terms and conditions of the Bylaws;

<!-- PROPOSED: Governance rights clarified per class — Investor Members are non-voting through Class 4 interest alone; a member holding any patron class votes as a patron member -->
(b) participation in Cooperative's governance and decision making: one vote per member holding any patron-class interest (Classes 1–3), regardless of shares held or capital contributed; Class 4 Investor Member interest is non-voting unless the member also holds a patron-class interest, in which case full voting rights apply through that patron interest;

<!-- DECIDE: Patronage language updated to reflect RegenHub's multi-factor formula, not a product/service-volume model. Financial Systems Committee to parameterize. -->
(c) patronage allocations as a Member, based on a formula established by the Board (with input from the Financial Systems Committee) weighting labor hours, revenue generation, cash contributions, and community building activity — as more fully described in Section 2.3 below; and

<!-- DECIDE: Community benefits to be defined per class. Proposed: shared physical space, cohort programming access, shared infrastructure (compute, tools), and venture ecosystem connectivity. -->
(d) access to Cooperative resources as defined for each membership class, which may include: physical space at 1515 Walnut St, Suite 200; Techne Institute cohort programming (101 / 201 / 301); shared computing and infrastructure; mentorship and knowledge sharing with Cooperative members and ventures; and participation in the venture ecosystem.

<!-- PROPOSED: Tax consent updated from Subchapter T (Meadow) to Subchapter K partnership pass-through -->
**2.3 Patronage Benefits.** Applicant may receive patronage allocations in accordance with and subject to the Bylaws and Policies or resolutions of the Board. Patronage allocations shall be based on a formula established by the Board, with input from the Financial Systems Committee, weighting:

<!-- DECIDE: These weights are proposed but not yet finalized. Financial Systems Committee convenes March 2, 2026. -->
- **Labor hours:** 40% (proposed)
- **Revenue generation:** 30% (proposed)
- **Cash contributions:** 20% (proposed)
- **Community building:** 10% (proposed)

<!-- DECIDE: Confirm whether "Financial Systems Committee recommendation" should be written into the agreement or kept as a Board resolution mechanism. -->
The Board has the right to change the patronage formula at its discretion, subject to the recommendation of the Financial Systems Committee and applicable provisions of the Bylaws.

**2.4 Membership Obligations of Applicant.** Applicant agrees to become a Member of the Cooperative in accordance with and subject to the terms and conditions of its Articles of Organization, Bylaws, Policies, Board resolutions, and this Agreement.

**2.5 Reputation, Integrity & Ethical Standards.** Applicant shall comply with all state, federal, and local laws, ordinances, rules and regulations, and shall act in an ethical, first-class and quality manner and according to the highest standards of integrity in connection with your business and affairs with the Cooperative. All information, reports, documents, etc. provided by Applicant to the Cooperative shall be accurate, complete, truthful and timely. If the Board determines, based on a good faith belief, that Applicant has inaccurately reported information, the Board may choose to terminate membership, in its sole discretion, for a breach of this section.

**2.6 Consent to Tax Treatment.** Applicant, by becoming a Member of the Cooperative and having received a copy of this Agreement, consents that the amount of any allocations with respect to Applicant's Patronage Activity (as that term is defined by the Bylaws)

(i) previously developed Intellectual Property proprietary to Applicant (or which such person licenses from third-parties) ("Applicant IP"), and

**3.3 "Intellectual Property"** means (a) all inventions (whether patentable or unpatentable and whether or not reduced to practice), all patents, patent applications, and patent disclosures, (b) all trademarks, service marks, trade dress, logos, trade names, and corporate names, (c) all copyrightable works, all copyrights, and all applications, registrations, and renewals in connection therewith, (d) all mask works, (e) all trade secrets and confidential business information, (f) all computer software (including data and related documentation), (g) all other proprietary rights, and (h) all copies and tangible embodiments thereof.

## 4. Confidentiality

**4.1** Each Party agrees to hold information provided to it by the other party as secret and strictly confidential, including, but not limited to, drawings, writings, know-how, marketing strategies, business operations and business systems, non-public financial information, and any other proprietary or sensitive information or material (collectively, "Confidential Information"). Confidential Information provided from one Party to the other will remain the sole property of the disclosing Party.

**4.2** Each Party agrees to use Confidential Information only to perform their respective obligations under this Agreement, the Governing Documents, Policies, and any other agreement among the Parties, and in accordance with applicable law.

**4.3** Each Party will use the same degree of care to protect the other Party's Confidential Information as it uses to safeguard its own Confidential Information but in no event less than a reasonable standard of care.

**4.4** Confidential Information does not include information that (i) is or becomes publicly available through no wrongful action of the receiving Party, (ii) is already in the possession of the receiving Party and not subject to an existing agreement of confidentiality, (iii) is received from a third party with no obligation of confidentiality, or (iv) is independently developed by the receiving Party without access to the other Party's Confidential Information.

## 5. Indemnification

Applicant hereby releases, and agrees to indemnify, defend, and hold harmless, the Cooperative and its directors, officers, members, employees, customers, contractors, personnel, agents, and representatives (collectively, "Affiliates") from and against any and all loss, cost, damages and/or expenses (including court costs and attorney's fees) incurred by Cooperative and/or its Affiliates as a result of (a) any negligence or misconduct of Applicant, (b) any breach of or inaccuracy in Applicant's representations and warranties hereunder, (c) any breach of this Agreement by Applicant, or (d) any termination of Applicant's rights under this Agreement due to Applicant's failure to comply with this Agreement, the Governing Documents, the Policies, or any resolution of the Board.

## 6. Termination

**6.1** In addition to the grounds for suspension or termination set forth in the Governing Documents, any of the following are additional grounds for suspension or termination:

> **6.1.1** Applicant fails to comply with any of the terms and provisions of this Agreement, the Governing Documents, the Policies, or any resolution or determination of the Board; or
>
> **6.1.2** Applicant makes a false statement concerning a representation or warranty made in this Agreement.

**6.2** Suspension or termination shall be carried out in accordance with the procedure set forth in the Governing Documents.

**6.3** Sections 3, 4, 5, 7, and 8, in addition to any other provisions which by their nature continue after termination, shall survive the termination of this Agreement.

## 7. Representations and Warranties

**7.1** Each Party represents and warrants to the other that (a) such Party has the right and ability to enter into this Agreement and to grant the rights granted herein, and (b) there is no outstanding or threatened litigation that would reasonably be expected to have a material adverse effect on such Party's ability to fulfill its obligations hereunder.

**7.2 Applicant's Additional Representations and Warranties.** Applicant further represents and warrants that: (a) Applicant's services will be performed in a professional, ethical, and efficient manner; (b) Applicant shall abide by applicable standards of ethical conduct; (c) Applicant's services and resulting Intellectual Property will not infringe any third party rights; (d) Applicant will abide by all applicable laws and regulations; and (e) Applicant will maintain appropriate safeguards to protect the security and confidentiality of all Confidential Information in its possession.
