## The Investment Thesis

RegenHub is the soil. Each venture is a seed. The basket is the seasonal harvest — what grows, what matures, what gets added each year. Rather than a SAFE into a single company at a fixed valuation, participation in RegenHub means investing in the farm.

The founding ventures — **Parachute**, **Postage**, and **Habitat** — each contribute equity into RegenHub as a cooperative gift. A portion of that equity is bundled into a single investable basket. New ventures are added as they mature. Your position compounds across the whole ecosystem rather than riding a single bet.

## The Agreement Stack

Participation in the RegenHub basket is structured through three layered documents. Together they constitute the legal and economic architecture of the transaction.

This structure is designed with our cooperative attorney (Jeff Pote, Pote Law Firm) to operate as **cooperative membership** — not a securities offering. Membership interests in a Colorado LCA are actively governed, patronage-based, and democratically controlled. They are not passive investment contracts. The intent is that no SEC registration or state securities filing is required; confirmations will be made with counsel before any transaction closes.

### 1. Bylaws
*The governance foundation.*

Establishes the cooperative's legal structure, member classes, capital account treatment, patronage formula, voting rights, and dissolution waterfall. The Investor Member class (Class 4) is defined here: non-voting, capital account-based, with participation in the basket instrument structured through the Member Agreement.

- **Status:** Proposed — values from formation meetings 1–8 integrated, Articles filed 2.6.26, Sub K analysis in progress
- **Attorney:** Jeff Pote (Pote Law Firm) — reviewing for 704(b) compliance
- **See:** [Decision Tree](https://roots-trust-lca.github.io/regenhub/) / [RegenHub Proposed Bylaws](https://github.com/Roots-Trust-LCA/regenhub/blob/main/RegenHub_Proposed_Bylaws.md)

### 2. Member Agreements
*The per-class operating contract.*

Operationalizes the bylaws at the level of each member class. For Investor Members, the Member Agreement specifies: the basket instrument (which ventures, what equity percentages), capital account treatment, the reserve mechanism, and the rights of cooperative members as the basket grows and new seasons are added.

For founding ventures (Parachute, Postage, Habitat), a separate Member Agreement documents the equity gift into RegenHub (10% of each venture) and the portion allocated to the basket (1–2%) versus held in reserve on RegenHub's balance sheet (8–9%).

- **Status:** Template to be drafted by Jeff Pote
- **Note:** This is the instrument layer — it is the agreement that makes the basket legible as a cooperative membership interest rather than a fund share

### 3. Term Sheet
*The first-season transaction record.*

The term sheet is intentionally minimal at this stage — two to four pages. Its purpose is to create a legal basis for receiving capital before the full return structure is determined. The member body (via the Financial Systems Committee) will define the specific return terms over time.

**Current shape:**

| Element | Value |

**Why no defined return at signing?** The Financial Systems Committee — convening March 2, 2026 — will parameterize the return terms in alignment with the full organizer body. Kevin and Jeremy are comfortable with this open structure. The cooperative retains full discretion.

**The basket structure** described in the Vision One-Pager represents the aspirational longer-term investment architecture — venture equity contributions, a shared fund, seasonal growth dynamics. That structure requires further legal definition via the Member Agreement and is the roadmap, not the initial transaction.
