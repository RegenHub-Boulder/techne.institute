01 — Who We Are

RegenHub & Techne

RegenHub, LCA is a Colorado Limited Cooperative Association — a public benefit entity with the stated purpose of cultivating scenius: the creative intelligence that emerges when skilled people share sustained proximity.

Eight founding organizers:

Benjamin Ross — civic technology; OpenCivics

We've operated from the second floor of 1515 Walnut since May 2024. Techne is what RegenHub is building next — a venture studio and project ecosystem opening from the third floor.

The Cooperative

RegenHub is the legal and organizational foundation. Eight organizers, committed investors, legal counsel in place. Filed as a Colorado LCA in February 2026.

The Studio

Techne is a DBA of RegenHub: technology education through LeanVibe.Build, venture incubation, and a rotating calendar of programs and convergences. Same cooperative, new capacity.

02 — The Lease Transfer

Aligning the Contract

Our current second-floor lease is held by West Software Services Inc., running through June 2027. We'd like to transfer it to RegenHub, LCA — the cooperative entity that now represents the group operating from this space.

Same people. Same commitment. Updated legal structure. The cooperative was filed in February 2026 and formalizes the working group that has been here for nearly two years.

03 — The Third Floor

Opening Techne

We'd like to lease the third floor and open it as Techne — an expanded workspace, program venue, and venture hub.

Three Capacities

Cowork
Coworking. An expanded daily workspace for members and affiliates. Desk memberships, meeting rooms, and shared infrastructure generate steady monthly revenue.
Programs
Programs. LeanVibe.Build — technology cohorts, cultural convergences like Sundance Boulder, civic workshops. A rotating seasonal calendar.
Ventures
Ventures. Early-stage technology ventures. Each contributes a minimum 1% annually back to the cooperative. A shared basket that compounds.

Who's Behind It

Every investor is also a participant in the work:

Kevin Owocki

Six-time founder, Managing Partner at Allo Capital. Has operated from 1515 Walnut's second floor for over a year.

Jeremy Wood

Motivated by access to the builder community and the creative energy emerging from this space.

Ethereum Foundation

Maintaining a Boulder presence and supporting open-source public goods infrastructure.

These investors cover the first year of the third-floor lease, giving cash flow time to grow and sustain it — the same pattern that proved out on the second floor. We're seeking a lease rate that reflects a long-term partnership — a number we'd like to arrive at together.

04 — The Longer View

A Path to Ownership

Over the next two to three years, we'd like to explore acquiring 1515 Walnut — a cooperatively-owned building that grows with what happens inside it.

We're not asking for a decision today. We're opening the conversation.

Third floor learning · Second floor ventures · Roof for gathering
A complete ecosystem from street to sky

As a first step, we'd be willing to fund an independent appraisal — a good-faith signal that we're serious about the long-term path, even as we work together on the near-term terms.

The Throughline

Every investor in this cooperative is also a participant in the work. Kevin works from the second floor. Jeremy Wood shows up at events. The Ethereum Foundation supports the ecosystem these people build in.

This is not outside capital looking for a return. It's people who are here because they believe in what Boulder can be.

We'd love to hear about your goals for the building, how you're thinking about its future, and whether there's a way to walk toward that future together.
