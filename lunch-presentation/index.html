The Plan for 1515 Walnut

March 11, 2026 · Lunch · Boulder

RegenHub is a public benefit entity with the stated purpose of cultivating scenius — the creative intelligence that emerges when skilled people share sustained proximity. We've operated from the second floor of 1515 Walnut since May 2024.

Techne is what RegenHub is building next — a venture studio and project ecosystem that opens to the public from the third floor. Techne supports technology education through LeanVibe.Build, incubates early-stage ventures, and hosts a rotating calendar of programs and convergences. It is a DBA of RegenHub: same cooperative, new capacity.

1. The Lease Transfer

Our current second-floor lease is held by West Software Services Inc., running through June 2027. We'd like to transfer it to RegenHub, LCA — the cooperative entity that now represents the group operating from this space.

This is largely administrative: same people, same commitment, updated legal structure. The cooperative was filed in February 2026 and formalizes the working group that has occupied this space for nearly two years.

Where things stand

RegenHub currently generates approximately $4,000–5,000 per month in operating cash flow, which covers the existing lease and enables the cooperative's day-to-day operations. The lease transfer aligns the contract with the entity doing the work.

2. The Third Floor

This is the primary near-term goal. We'd like to lease the third floor and open it as Techne — an expanded workspace, program venue, and venture hub. Three capacities define what it becomes:

Coworking. An expanded daily workspace for members and affiliates. Desk memberships, meeting rooms, and shared infrastructure generate steady monthly revenue and bring a working community into the building.
Programs. A rotating seasonal calendar. First up: LeanVibe.Build — helping people rediscover the craft of code. The space is designed for what comes next: technology cohorts, cultural convergences like Sundance Boulder, civic workshops, and programs at the intersection of bioregioning and civics.
Ventures. The cooperative incubates early-stage technology ventures. Each contributes a minimum 1% annually back to the cooperative, creating a shared basket that compounds over time.
Financing the third floor

We have over $130,000 in committed capital from investors who are also participants in the work:

Kevin Owocki — $50,000 committed. Six-time founder, Managing Partner at Allo Capital. Has operated from 1515 Walnut's second floor for over a year.

Jeremy — $50,000 committed. Motivated by access to the builder community and the creative energy emerging from this space.

Ethereum Foundation — $30,000 committed. Interested in maintaining a Boulder presence and supporting open-source public goods infrastructure.

These investors cover the first year of the third-floor lease, giving cash flow time to grow and sustain it — the same pattern that proved out on the second floor. We're seeking a lease rate that reflects a long-term partnership: $5,000 per month, with immediate occupancy by a stable, mission-aligned tenant already in the building.

3. The Longer View

We want to be transparent about where we hope this relationship goes. Over the next two to three years, we'd like to explore a path toward acquiring 1515 Walnut — a cooperatively-owned building that grows with what happens inside it.

We're not asking for a decision on this now. What we'd welcome is the conversation: understanding your goals for the building, sharing ours, and seeing whether there's a structure that works for everyone over time.

The stepwise model

The pattern is deliberate: visionary investors cover the first year of each expansion, then operating revenue grows to sustain it.

Second floor — covered. Cash flow sustains the lease today.

Third floor — investors above are ready to cover year one. Cash flow grows from the expanded space, programs, and memberships.

Building — at roughly $2.5 million (pending appraisal), debt service would run approximately $11,000–12,000 per month. That's about three times where we are today. With the third floor open and programs running, it becomes a realistic horizon rather than a speculative one.

As a first step, we'd be willing to fund an independent appraisal — a good-faith signal that we're serious about the long-term path, even as we work together on the near-term terms.

The Throughline

Every investor in this cooperative is also a participant in the work. Kevin works from the second floor. Jeremy shows up at events. The Ethereum Foundation supports the ecosystem these people build in. This is not outside capital looking for a return — it's people who are here because they believe in what Boulder can be.

That's the kind of relationship we'd like to build with you: not a transaction, but a partnership where the growth of what happens inside 1515 Walnut benefits everyone connected to it.

We'd love to hear about your goals for the building, how you're thinking about its future, and whether there's a way to walk toward that future together.

We come prepared and we come in good faith.
