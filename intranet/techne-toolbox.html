§1

What Techne is, and who it is for.

Techne is infrastructure for organizations that want to run themselves deliberately. Organizations that want to make their decisions traceable, their commitments legible, their learning reusable, and their internal economy visible beyond what shows up on a balance sheet. It was built at RegenHub, a Colorado public benefit cooperative, and it is offered to any organization that recognizes the same problems in its own work.

The market for this is wider than it first appears. Cooperatives and commons-based organizations are the obvious fit. They have always needed tools that traditional enterprise software could not provide. But the deeper claim is that commons-based, participatory, iterative practice is not only ethically compelling, it is practically superior for navigating complexity, which is the condition every modern organization now operates under. Traditional companies running innovation programs, associations managing member engagement, nonprofits coordinating distributed teams, and on-chain networks experimenting with new governance forms all face problems Techne was built to address.

What distinguishes Techne from a typical SaaS toolkit is the substrate. Every tool speaks the same underlying language, the Resource-Event-Agent ontology specified in our companion document. This is what makes the tools compose rather than merely coexist. When you add a second tool, it does not need to be told what agents exist or what resources are in play. It already knows, because both tools are reading the same event log.

The tools do not need to be integrated. They were never separated in the first place.
§2

The shape of the toolbox.

The toolbox has two tiers. The lower tier is three foundations that expose the REA substrate directly: Treasury, People, and Agreements. Each can be adopted and used alone. The upper tier is four lenses that sit across specific combinations of foundations, each one a named capability an organization can recognize and want: Patronage, Reconciliation, Governance, and Learning. Lenses do not exist separately from their foundations. Adopting a lens activates the foundations it rests on.

Three lenses sit along the edges of the triangle, each composed from the two foundations it touches. Patronage lives between Treasury and People, because it needs to know both what flowed and who was involved. Reconciliation lives between Treasury and Agreements, because it tracks commitment against fulfillment. Governance lives between People and Agreements, because it asks who is empowered to bind us and what they have bound us to. Learning sits at the center because it draws on all three foundations at once, turning the whole event log into an institutional memory that other organizations can later benefit from.

§3

The foundations, specified.

Tier One · Foundations Three primitives over the REA substrate

Each foundation exposes one face of the substrate directly. Each has standalone value. Each becomes the condition for one or more lenses when paired with the others.

FOUNDATION 01
Treasury
REAResources
Stockflow Events

"Multi-capital accounting for the whole economy of your organization."

Standalone value

Track any resource whose flow matters: cash, labor hours, credits, tokens, space-hours, carbon, care work, anything. Record events with signed quantities. Balances are derived from the immutable event log. Generate conventional double-entry reports for accountants, tax authorities, and grant-makers, while keeping the richer underlying reality visible to members who need it. Purpose-built to support partnership tax regimes like Subchapter K, including 704(b) book capital accounts alongside the tax view.

First adopted by

Organizations that recognize value in things beyond cash: cooperatives, mutual aid networks, community land trusts, nonprofits with in-kind economies. Also traditional companies wanting better visibility into innovation credits, employee contribution, or ESG metrics, things their general ledger cannot easily represent but their people care about.

FOUNDATION 02
People
REAAgents
Classes · Credentials

"Who is in, how they show up, and what that means over time."

Standalone value

A complete registry of the people, groups, and organizations participating in your organization. Define classes with distinct rights and obligations. Onboard new agents through defined flows. Issue and verify credentials. Track tenure, status, and contribution history. Give every member a view of their own standing they can query without steward mediation. Works on its own as a modern member registry with first-class support for nested organizations and non-human stewards.

First adopted by

Any organization with a nontrivial membership structure: cooperatives, associations, member-owned businesses, professional networks, mutual aid groups, unions, on-chain networks. Also traditional companies that want to model their workforce and partner network with more precision than an HRIS provides, especially for work that crosses legal entity boundaries.

FOUNDATION 03
Agreements
REACommitments
Contracts · Events

"The space between a handshake and a legal contract, made legible."

Standalone value

Draft, negotiate, and track mutual commitments. The promises that make up most organizational life but that rarely warrant formal legal contracts. See what is outstanding, what has been fulfilled, what has been revised, and who agreed to what. Bundle related commitments into contracts. Route disputed agreements into resolution processes without erasing the original record. Works alone as a lightweight alternative to contract management and institutional memory at once.

First adopted by

Organizations that run on relationships: cooperatives, partner networks, service exchanges, working groups, professional guilds, restorative justice practices. Also innovation teams and project offices at traditional companies who need to track cross-functional commitments without generating legal overhead.

§4

The lenses, specified.

Tier Two · Lenses Four named capabilities composed from foundations

A lens is a named capability that presupposes a specific combination of foundations. Adopting a lens activates its foundations automatically. You never have to install them separately. This is the practical consequence of building on one substrate: composition is free.

LENS 01
Patronage
EDGETreasury
× People

"Who contributed what, and what came back."

RequiresTreasury + People
What the lens does

Makes contribution legible at the level of the individual member. Every Treasury event is already tagged with its agents, so Patronage can compute, in real time, what each member has given and received across every resource type the organization tracks. This is what turns surplus allocation from an argument into a calculation, and what makes invisible labor visible. For cooperatives that have elected Subchapter K partnership taxation, Patronage is also where 704(b) book capital accounts live, ensuring that the book side of the ledger can satisfy the substantial economic effect test the allocations have to meet.

First adopted by

Cooperatives with partnership tax elections, mutual aid networks, commons stewardship groups, any organization that wants to distribute returns based on contribution rather than ownership. Also grant-funded organizations that need to show funders how contributed labor was tracked and valued.

LENS 02
Reconciliation
EDGETreasury
× Agreements

"What we said we would do, against what actually moved."

RequiresTreasury + Agreements
What the lens does

Closes the loop between promise and delivery. Every Agreement lives next to a Treasury event log that can tell you, at any moment, which commitments have been fulfilled, which are pending, and which have quietly drifted past their expected close. Reconciliation also enforces policy constraints at the ledger layer: a resource movement that would violate an approved spending policy does not post silently, it gets flagged for resolution. Shortfalls surface before they become disputes.

First adopted by

Partnership networks, grant-funded organizations, fiscal sponsors, any organization whose commitments do not always turn into actions. Also project offices at traditional companies that need to track cross-functional commitments against actual delivery without generating legal or procurement overhead.

LENS 03
Governance
EDGEPeople
× Agreements

"How we decide together, and how we remember what we decided."

RequiresPeople + Agreements
What the lens does

Turns policy-making and decision processes into first-class, versioned, auditable objects. Propose a policy. Run a structured decision process (consent, consensus, majority, sociocratic rounds, or whatever your organization actually uses). Vote with weights derived from agent class. Preserve the full history of who proposed what, who weighed in, and what was decided, with effective dates and version lineage. When disagreement rises, route it into an appropriate resolution path without erasing the original record. This is the lens that answers the question every mature organization eventually asks itself: "wait, why did we decide that?"

First adopted by

Cooperatives, sociocracies, member-owned businesses, on-chain networks, nonprofits with active boards. Also traditional companies adopting distributed decision-making, Teal practices, or governance innovations they want to track rigorously rather than reconstruct from memory.

LENS 04
Learning
CENTERAll three
foundations

"What you figured out, captured before it evaporates."

RequiresAny foundation (benefits from all)
What the lens does

Makes organizational knowledge durable and reusable. Document what worked, what did not, and why. Link learning artifacts directly to the events, commitments, and decisions they came from, so that future members can trace a lesson back to its source and forward to its consequences. Export anonymized pattern libraries so that one organization's hard-won insight can become another's starting point. This is also where public benefit reporting lives for organizations that owe an account of their work to funders, members, or the public, because a learning artifact linked to its evidence is exactly the kind of report those audiences need.

First adopted by

Research collectives, design studios, cooperatives committed to teaching other cooperatives, professional communities, and any team whose institutional memory walks out the door when someone leaves. Also public-benefit organizations that need to show their work to funders and the communities they serve.

§5

How organizations enter the toolbox.

Because lenses activate their foundations, an organization can enter the toolbox at either tier. Starting with a foundation gives you the most flexibility and the smallest upfront commitment. Starting with a lens gives you a named capability the same day you install, with its foundations quietly turning on underneath. Below are three common entry patterns. None is prescriptive.

The important property is that whatever you adopt first starts paying off immediately. A foundation gives you raw capability. A lens gives you a named capability with its foundations quietly activated underneath. And whatever you add next compounds automatically, because the substrate was there all along. No organization has to commit to the full stack on day one. Most should not.

§6

How Techne is delivered.

Techne is offered in two forms. Client organizations can run the hosted version, where RegenHub operates the infrastructure and clients configure the tools for their own context under their own brand. Or they can self-host from open-source reference implementations of the core substrate, with paid offerings for managed operation, upgrades, and direct support from the people who also build it.

The hosted arrangement gives a client organization infrastructure without operating it, cooperative methodology without needing a cooperative background to implement it, and real modularity without the usual integration tax. It also means that when RegenHub improves a tool, because we are running it ourselves and encountering the same edge cases, every hosted client benefits from that improvement on the same schedule. The self-hosted path preserves the option for organizations that need full sovereignty over their data or infrastructure, without breaking the shared substrate.

§7

The through-line.

Techne is cooperative wisdom in a form that any organization can use. That phrase deserves to be unpacked.

Cooperative wisdom, because the hardest organizational problems (how to make distributed decisions legibly, how to track value that is not money, how to handle conflict without erasing it, how to compound learning across time) are problems the cooperative tradition has been working on for over a century. Not always well, but persistently, and with a clarity of intention that most enterprise software has never needed to develop.

Any organization, because those problems are no longer specific to cooperatives. A traditional corporation running distributed innovation programs, a nonprofit coordinating across chapters, an on-chain network trying to operationalize its charter: all of them are confronting questions the cooperative movement has been wrestling with for generations. Techne is how we make the answers available without requiring anyone to adopt the legal form.

And in a form that any organization can use matters because the answers are not useful as theory. They are only useful as tools you can pick up, install, and start running. That is what the toolbox is for, and that is why it has two tiers: foundations for those who want to build their own way up, lenses for those who know what capability they need and want it the day they install.

Invitation

Start with one. Add more when you see what it is like.

We built Techne to run RegenHub, and we are offering it to other organizations because the problems we solved are not ours alone. They belong to anyone trying to build something deliberate, participatory, and legible to its own members. The toolbox is open. Pick a foundation, pick a lens, or pick the one thing that sounds most useful to you right now. Each tool is already valuable by itself. The next one compounds. By the time you have three or four of them running, you are on an operational grammar designed from the ground up for organizations that learn.
