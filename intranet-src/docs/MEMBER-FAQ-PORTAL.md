# Member FAQ — Understanding Your Capital Account

---

## What is a "book balance"?

Your book balance is the economic fair value of your cooperative account under GAAP accounting. It represents your current share of the cooperative's net assets, based on how much you've contributed (labor, revenue, capital, community participation) and any distributions you've received.

This is the number that matters for understanding your economic ownership — what your stake in the cooperative is worth today.

---

## What is the "tax capital balance"?

The tax capital balance is your account balance under IRC 704(b) / Subchapter K — the federal tax rules that govern how cooperative income and loss is allocated among members.

This number appears on your K-1 (Schedule K-1, Form 1065) and is what matters for your tax return. It differs from the book balance when there are unrealized gains, depreciation, or differences between GAAP and tax treatment.

If the two numbers differ significantly and you're not sure why, ask a steward.

---

## How is my patronage allocation calculated?

Each quarter, the cooperative calculates allocations using this formula:

| Component | Weight | Based on |
|-----------|--------|----------|
| Labor | 40% | Your active participation hours |
| Revenue | 30% | Your patronage transactions with the cooperative |
| Capital | 20% | Capital you've deployed to cooperative ventures |
| Community | 10% | Civic and community contributions |

If you joined mid-quarter, labor and capital components are prorated by days active. Revenue and community are allocated for the full quarter.

## I think my balance is wrong. What do I do?

Email **steward@techne.studio** with:
- Your name
- The quarter you believe is incorrect
- What you think the correct figure should be and why (e.g., "I worked 120 hours in Q1 2026, but the labor allocation seems too low")

The steward will cross-reference against the patronage engine records and correct any errors.

---

## What is a "qualified" vs. "non-qualified" allocation?

Under IRC 1382, a cooperative can make two kinds of patronage distributions:

- **Qualified:** Written notice to members within 8.5 months of year-end. Members must treat 20% of the qualified amount as ordinary income, even if not received in cash. These are deductible by the cooperative.
- **Non-qualified:** No current tax obligation for the member. The amount is not deductible by the cooperative until the member actually receives it.

Your K-1 will show the breakdown. Most of your allocation will be qualified notices.
